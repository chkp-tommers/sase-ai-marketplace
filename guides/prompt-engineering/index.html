Prompt Engineering

What is Prompt Engineering?

Prompt engineering is the art and science of crafting instructions for large language models (LLMs) to achieve the most accurate, relevant, and useful results. It involves structuring prompts, providing context, and using advanced techniques to guide the model's behavior and output.

Best Practices

  • Be clear and direct in your instructions.
  • Assign roles using system prompts (e.g., "You are a helpful assistant...").
  • Provide examples (multishot prompting) to show the model what you expect.
  • Use templates and variables for consistency and flexibility.
  • Encourage step-by-step reasoning (chain-of-thought).
  • Use XML tags or other structure to control output format.
  • Prefill responses or chain prompts for complex workflows.
  • Leverage long context and extended thinking for deep tasks.

Adapted from Claude 4 Best Practices and applicable to all LLMs.

Advanced Techniques

  • Multishot Prompting: Provide several input/output examples to guide the model.
  • Chain-of-Thought (CoT): Ask the model to reason step by step.
  • XML Tags: Use tags to enforce structure and clarity in responses.
  • Chaining Prompts: Break complex tasks into multiple, linked prompts.
  • Prefill Responses: Seed the model's output with a starting point.
